Guiding Principles
Reader First, Always
Every piece starts from the reader's problem. Internal knowledge (orchestration funnel, Pathfinder, services) shapes HOW we write, never WHAT the headline says. The reader arrives at Researchpreneurs on their own.
Every Piece Connects to a Real Service
No vanity content. Every blog, newsletter, and video connects to something Researchpreneurs actually offers — Pathfinder, fractional experts, full orchestration, partner program. If it doesn't connect, it doesn't get made.
Channels Differentiate, Not Duplicate
Blog educates (the answer). Newsletter provokes (the itch). YouTube demonstrates (the proof). A reader could follow all three and never feel like they're getting the same thing twice.
Balanced Funnel, Not Just Education
3 BOFU articles that convert. 3 MOFU articles that educate. 2 TOFU articles that attract. The blog isn't just for awareness — it's a conversion engine with content at every stage of the buying decision.
Key Questions for Monday
To unlock the best content
1 Can I get a Pathfinder walkthrough this week? I need to understand inputs, outputs, and the result format to write content around it.
2 Do you have data on average project costs, timelines, or client savings I can reference in content? Even ballpark ranges help.
3 What are the most common frustrations clients mention about previous R&D outsourcing experiences before finding RP?
4 Which industry verticals have the most projects on the platform — MedTech, cleantech, agrifood? This determines which vertical blog I write.
5 Can you walk me through the partner model — how it works, what partners receive, and whether there are partners I can reference?
6 Would the team be open to a contributed article for an external publication like EU-Startups or Sifted under the founder's name?
7 What's the review/approval process for content? Quick turnaround matters for a 2-per-week blog cadence.
8 Should all content drive to Pathfinder as the primary CTA, or do we vary by audience and content type?
